Did I just create a new ticket

Update HydraDB

First inform HydraDB that a new ticket was created.

Click on Utilities and then Bug Utilities in the HydraDB

Enter the new ticket you just created, Select Tag as New, ensure that you have correct Run# and click on Run Now

Bisect the new JIRA

Now its time to bisect and figure out what needs

Once you have created the new ticket and labelled the failure as such, a new section will appear in the test run dashboard.

Click on the thunder storm icon next to it which will open Bisect It Now page

Select a couple of previous commits. Ensure that it covers at least a week worth of commits.

There are option to choose the JDK etc.

Click on Generate Script

As the bisect operations start, you can view the status in http://hydradb-sandbox.gemfire.pivotal.io:9090/

The label will point to the bisect operation, ticket number and the user.

After the bisect tests are completed

Two things may happen

  1. You figured out the commit that caused the failure. Contact the author of the commit and ask them to revert the commit. This is non-negotiable.
  1. If you are not able to pinpoint it to a particular commit, that means that it needs to be investigated, hence mark the ticket as release-blocker-1.xx . Replace the last 1.xx with the version number of develop in the JIRA ticket you had just created

This will trigger a message on #caching-releases . Leadership and anchors will try to assign resources to resolve this issue.
